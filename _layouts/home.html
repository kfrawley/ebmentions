The Letters of
Marie Mancini

In the spring of 1672, Italian noblewoman Marie Mancini became aware that her husband, the preeminant Lorenzo Onofrio Colonna, intended to poison her. To escape, she fled Rome with her sister, Hortense, to France where the two hoped to win the protection of King Louis XIV (Marie's former lover), which would allow them to live independently from their husbands.

However, Marie and Hortense were not welcomed to court by the king. They were forced to find their own way to subsist independently.
