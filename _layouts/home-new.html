The Letters of

Marie Mancini

The historical study of women’s lives often requires the piecing together of disparate and partial evidence mined from archives.

Marie Mancini (1639-1715) was famous as the first love of Louis XIV of France, and later she and her sister Hortense became scandalous celebrities by running away from their husbands and traveling unaccompanied through much of Western Europe. Marie’s unpublished letters to her estranged husband and others reside in an Italian archive. This project will publish them online.
